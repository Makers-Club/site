    The Problem

    Early-career web developers often require 6 months of training and over $50k before they can contribute meaningful code. Bootcamp students learn programming languages. College students learn computer science theory. Neither begin their career ready to make serious webapps, but isn’t that the point?

    The Solution

    A social, team-focused coding platform that provides insights from developers of real-world projects, guides you through recreating your own versions of them, tests your code automatically, and showcases your work for employers. There are no toy-torials here. Only real work to prepare you for the real world.

    Our Purpose

    Maker Teams exists to eliminate uncertainty for software students and junior developers. We know that programming and making products are two very different things. We've been there. Come join or create a team. Allow practice, guidance, and teamwork to provide answers to the questions that documentation can't help you with.

    • "Where do I even begin?"
    • "Do I need an API?"
    • "Should this function be here or there?"
    • "How do I avoid tech-debt?"
    • "How do large teams affect coding style?"
    • "What questions should I ask clients?"
    • ...and the list goes on and on.
